WHAT IS BULLYING

According to the dictionary, bullying is to do harm, intimidate, or coerce (someone perceived as vulnerable). There are different ways to harm a person. For instance, these are examples of bullying:

  1. Physical bullying: hitting, slapping or pushing someone.
  2. Verbal bullying: name calling, gossiping or threatening someone.
  3. Non-verbal abuse: hand signs or text messages.
  4. Emotional abuse: threatening, intimidating or humiliating someone.
  5. Exclusion: ignoring or isolating someone.
  6. Cyberbullying: someone uses technology to harass, threaten, embarrass, or target another person.
